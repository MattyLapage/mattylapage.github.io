Biography

I am a Brooklyn based psychology nerd with an acute interest in Human Computer Interaction and they way user data can inform design practices. In 2011 I received my Bachelors in Psychology from Cornell University, returned to Cornell for a 2012 Masters of Professional Studies in Information Science. I have spent the year since working as a full stack web developer at Next Jump Inc, a NYC based e-commerce platform, and am now seeking out new opportunities in the realms of User Experience and Product design.

Skills

I have a mixed background in experimental psychology, computer science, and the wonderful intersection of HCI. This includes:

  • Experience with heuristic evaluation, user surveys and interviews, eye tracking technologies, and rapid prototyping (digital and physical).
  • Self-taught skills in Adobe Creative Suite, Prototype and JQuery, Visual Studio, MSSQL and Jekyll.
  • Fluency in Python, Java, Javascript, PHP, CSS, HTML, SQL, and Spanish (though that last one is getting rusty)
  • Some limited experience with Ruby on Rails, NLTK, D3, and Weka
  • Research work on motivation psychology in the context of online tools, data analysis on digital means of reflection , long distance coordination and interruptions , with some light work in visual perception and cognitive film theory.

Work History

Next Jump:

From August 2012 through October 2013 I worked at Next Jump as a Web Applications Developer. As a full stack developer in a six person pod, I developed consumer facing sites in PHP, CSS and Javascript with occasional back-end scripting in SQL. For the most part this involved updates to our onsite shopping portal at OO.com. I coordinated cross team with our Customer Service and Design teams, as well as communicating directly with customers, to locate and repair UX pain points and rebuild our onsite Help center by better understanding user needs.

I worked with a three person team on the ongoing rebranding of the Next Jump Corporate blog using Github Pages and Jekyll, including design of the blog itself, copy editing of posts, and PR.

Took on a leading role in organizing weekly in-office social events and occasional off-site events, and became the first official Next Jump NY kegmaster responsible for keeping the lounge kegerators stocked and running 24/7.

Research Assistant (Various projects), Cornell University:

May 09 - May 11: Designed and implemented OpenMessenger, a C# chat client that used ambient computer activity and eye tracking to gauge the status of teammates with a goal of reducing the effects of digital interruptions in long distance teams.

Aug 09 - May 11: Database analyst for Pensieve, a social network scraping tool. Coauthor on Pensieve: Supporting Everyday Reminiscence, Proceedings of CHI 2010.

May 10 - 12: Web designer for Goalmometer, a task manager geared towards motivation. Created and tested a series of offline prototypes for user evaluation, and ran surveys and interviews with users over a three month trial period of our system.
